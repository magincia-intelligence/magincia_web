## Top of the brief

- **Norton: "rule by email" in higher education policy (20 May).** Using FOI-obtained ministerial correspondence, Andrew Norton argues the government has imposed significant higher education policies — the $50m over-enrolment fund and a set of governance disclosure requirements — on universities through ministerial emails to vice-chancellors rather than through legislation. Emails were sent 11 December 2025; VCs had until 14 January 2026 to commit. Six of the nine recipient universities are in Melbourne or Sydney. Norton's core objection: "proper process requires policies be announced, debated, enacted legislatively, and made publicly accessible before implementation." ([Norton, 20 May](https://andrewnorton.id.au/2026/05/20/rule-by-email-in-higher-education-policy-over-enrolment-and-governance/))

- **Budget 2026-27: sector response (19 May).** The PIE surveyed sector reaction to the 12 May budget. Welcomed: the National Resilience and Science Council, Horizon Europe association, increased MRFF disbursements, research specialisation support, and faster trade recognition for skilled migrants. Criticised: termination of Australia's Economic Accelerator. Universities Australia CEO Luke Sheehy said universities "are bearing the brunt of increased regulation and costs at a time when investment in teaching and research is not keeping up." The Go8 described Horizon Europe association as "nation-building and strategic investment." ([The PIE, 19 May](https://thepienews.com/australian-budget-puts-research-and-skills-in-focus/))

- **ASQA provider freeze now in force.** As of 19 May, ASQA has suspended new international student provider registration applications in the VET and ELICOS sectors for 12 months, to 19 May 2027. Government schools, TAFEs, and Table A universities are exempt. ([The PIE, 18 May](https://thepienews.com/australia-freezes-new-overseas-student-provider-registrations/))

- **ANU governance: Coaldrake panel under way.** TEQSA's voluntary undertaking requiring a Coaldrake-chaired Chancellor selection panel remains in place. Norton's 11 May critique on the legal risk under s17(3) of the ANU Act remains the sharpest public commentary. ([Norton, 11 May](https://andrewnorton.id.au/2026/05/11/teqsas-response-to-the-anus-governance-issues/))

## Funding & system architecture

- **Budget 2026-27 higher education measures** (handed down 12 May): Horizon Europe association, National Resilience and Science Council, increased MRFF, research specialisation support, and apprenticeship incentives for small employers from July 2027. Australia's Economic Accelerator program was terminated. ([The PIE, 19 May](https://thepienews.com/australian-budget-puts-research-and-skills-in-focus/))
- **Over-enrolment fund ($50m, August 2025).** Norton's FOI material confirms eligibility required more than 5% over-enrolment on 2024 data; individual university payments were capped at $10m; Monash and UTS received the largest allocations. ([Norton, 20 May](https://andrewnorton.id.au/2026/05/20/rule-by-email-in-higher-education-policy-over-enrolment-and-governance/))
- **Managed Growth Funding System** — 2026 is the transition year; full commencement 2027. The $50m Structural Adjustment Fund opens 1 July.

## International education

- **ASQA freeze — context.** The provider freeze has been in effect since 19 May. Sector backdrop: ELICOS commencements fell 35% year-on-year in 2025; visa refusal rates reached 69% for Nepal and 42% for India in early 2026. Julian Hill: "Suspending new registrations to teach international students VET or English language onshore is not a decision taken lightly." ([The PIE, 18 May](https://thepienews.com/australia-freezes-new-overseas-student-provider-registrations/))
- **Budget — skills.** Faster trade recognition for skilled migrants, and training investment aligned to priority sectors including AUKUS and aged care, were announced in the 12 May budget. ([The PIE, 19 May](https://thepienews.com/australian-budget-puts-research-and-skills-in-focus/))
- **2026 NPL remains 295,000 places** (+25,000 on 2025); traffic-light processing model in effect since 14 November 2025.

## Regulator

- **TEQSA.** The ANU voluntary undertaking remains the live action item; no update.
- **ATEC.** Norton's 20 May post references an August 2025 Clare letter to interim ATEC outlining the over-enrolment fund; ATEC's interim statement of strategic priorities was published 6 May. ([Norton, 6 May](https://andrewnorton.id.au/2026/05/06/atecs-interim-statement-of-strategic-priorities/))
