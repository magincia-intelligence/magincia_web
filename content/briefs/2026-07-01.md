## Top of the brief
- **Home Affairs raises student visa to $2,500 and introduces separate ELICOS tier at $2,050, with Temporary Graduate visa up 25% to $5,750 (30 June).** Australia increased student visa (subclass 500) fees from $2,000 to $2,500 overnight — a 25 per cent rise — while creating a new lower-cost ELICOS pricing tier at $2,050, the first time English-language study has carried a distinct visa price point. The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) rose from $4,600 to $5,750, also up 25 per cent. Jesse Gardner-Russell, Council of Australian Postgraduate Associations president: "This is a fee on the exit door, not the front door. If the aim is to manage who comes in, charging the people already on their way out is the wrong instrument." The increases compound an already elevated cost-of-access environment: 2026 NPL stands at 295,000 and refusal rates remain 69% (Nepal) and 42% (India). ([The PIE, 30 June](https://thepienews.com/student-visa-fees-rise-again-as-australia-adds-elicos-pricing-tier/))

## Funding & system architecture
- **Norton: Universities Accord Bill prohibits ATEC from allocating to specific courses, contradicting its workforce mandate; twelve-criteria AGA a structural uncertainty for universities (30 June).** In the second of his Universities Accord Bill explainer series, Andrew Norton identifies what he describes as a fundamental contradiction: section 30-20(3) of the bill prohibits ATEC from allocating Commonwealth Supported Places to specific courses (other than designated ones), despite the commission's stated mandate to address national skills and workforce priorities. Norton also finds the Additional Growth Allocation — the discretionary component of each university's domestic student profile — is governed by twelve listed criteria including student demand, market dynamics, sector sustainability, equity performance, and delivery capacity, making forward planning difficult. A further structural risk: universities temporarily under-enrolled in 2027 face reduced core student load calculations in 2028–2029, since the base figure is the lower of their 2025 verified enrolment or a notional CSP position. ([Norton, 30 June](https://andrewnorton.id.au/2026/06/30/the-proposed-new-funding-system-part-2-atec-allocations-of-commonwealth-supported-places-to-public-universities/))

## International education
- **Clare launches Social Cohesion Education Hub with $6 million over five years and 50 curated resources for schools (30 June).** The Albanese Government opened a national online platform consolidating existing antisemitism and social cohesion materials for schools and educators, with approximately half of the initial 50 resources focused on antisemitism specifically. The hub is delivered by Education Services Australia, quality-assured with input from the Office of the Special Envoy to Combat Antisemitism, and connected to the Antisemitism Education Taskforce midpoint report also released 30 June by Taskforce chair David Gonski AC. ACARA is separately reviewing school curriculum to strengthen antisemitism education and Australian values teaching. Clare: "There is no place for the poison of antisemitism anywhere in our society." ([Clare, 30 June](https://ministers.education.gov.au/clare/social-cohesion-education-hub))
