## Top of the brief

- **Universities Australia: 2027 NPL hold "a big number on paper" without broader policy reform to make Australia competitive (3 July).** CEO Luke Sheehy welcomed the government's confirmation that the National Planning Level will hold at 295,000 for 2027 — the same level as 2026 — but was direct about its limits: "A big number on paper means little if the policies behind it make it harder to deliver in practice." UA argues current settings make Australia "harder, more expensive and less certain" for international students, warning that without reform the 2027 allocation will fail to deliver the economic and skills outcomes the $55 billion, 250,000-job sector requires. The release — UA's first substantive public reaction to Clare's 3 July package — was not caught in the Friday or Saturday brief windows. ([Universities Australia, 3 July](https://universitiesaustralia.edu.au/media-item/student-numbers-steady-but-policy-settings-holding-australia-back/))

- **Government holds 2027 National Planning Level at 295,000; no active provider to receive a lower 2027 allocation than in 2026 (3 July).** Education Minister Jason Clare, Home Affairs Minister Tony Burke, Skills Minister Andrew Giles and Assistant Minister Julian Hill confirmed the NPL will remain unchanged next year, with government citing commencements running 8% below 2025 and 13% below 2019 as evidence international student flows are on track to sit below the cap. Regional universities retain last year's stronger allocations; TAFE remains exempt under the "TAFE at the Heart" policy; Pacific, Timor-Leste and Australian government scholarship holders keep priority visa processing. Ministerial Direction continues to shape flows via visa prioritisation, and — subject to passage of the Universities Accord bill — ATEC will take over higher education international allocations from 2027. ([The PIE, 3 July](https://thepienews.com/australia-maintains-2027-international-student-planning-level-at-295000/))

## Funding & system architecture

## International education

- **2027 NPL package introduces ASEAN and non-award visa fee concessions alongside standing Pacific/Timor-Leste settings.** In the same announcement holding the NPL at 295,000, government confirmed the new visa fee schedule (student subclass 500 at $2,500, ELICOS tier at $2,050) also carries lower fees for applicants from ASEAN countries and for non-award courses, in addition to the retained lower fees for Pacific nations and Timor-Leste. Growth-seeking universities will continue to be assessed against government priorities including Southeast Asia engagement and adequate student accommodation. ([The PIE, 3 July](https://thepienews.com/australia-maintains-2027-international-student-planning-level-at-295000/))
