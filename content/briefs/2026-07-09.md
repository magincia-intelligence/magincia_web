## Top of the brief
- **RMIT UP CEO Heinrich: ELICOS sector faces existential threat as 285% four-year visa fee rise rivals tuition costs; Australia risks losing diplomatic soft power (8 July).** Jake Heinrich, CEO of RMIT University Pathways, argues in The PIE that English language colleges are "Australia's front door" and a long-undervalued soft-power asset now under existential pressure. The student visa application fee has risen from $650 (2022) to $2,500 — a 285% increase in four years — with ELICOS students paying a distinct $2,050 tier. Heinrich argues the cost "now rivals or exceeds tuition expenses," fundamentally altering prospective students' decision to study in Australia. The community dimension is stark: "Behind every declining intake is an English teacher whose classes become smaller, an administrator whose role becomes uncertain, a homestay family with an empty room and a local café serving fewer customers." His central concern is diplomatic: students who complete ELICOS pathways become "business leaders, educators, entrepreneurs and government officials" and long-term ambassadors for Australia — goodwill that the current fee trajectory is discarding. ([The PIE, 8 July](https://thepienews.com/australias-quiet-achiever-english-language-colleges-at-a-crossroads/))
