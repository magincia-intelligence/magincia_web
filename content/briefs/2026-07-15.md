## Top of the brief
- **Norton and Xu: postgraduate CSPs now 58% of public university places — first time since the 1990s — driven by JRG framework and post-COVID undergraduate slump; ATEC caps and enrolment recovery put the gains at risk (14 July).** In new research co-authored with Ren-Hao Xu, Andrew Norton charts a structural reversal in postgraduate education: Commonwealth Supported Places have grown from 5.9% to 8.4% of all CSPs since 2019, with postgraduate CSPs now comprising 58% of public university postgraduate enrolments. The Job-ready Graduates framework abolished discrete postgraduate CSP allocations (except medicine) and allowed universities to redirect underused undergraduate capacity during COVID-era demand slumps. IT postgraduate CSPs tripled between 2019 and 2024; nursing's CSP share rose from 66% to 88%; and medical postgraduate CSPs grew 93%. Norton warns of structural fragility as undergraduate demand recovers and ATEC imposes tighter allocation caps: "We give Melbourne and Brisbane examples where one university competed on price using CSPs in IT programs, forcing competitors to follow." Only medical postgraduate CSPs carry statutory protection; over $1 billion was spent on under-enrolment payments across 2021–2024. ([Norton, 14 July](https://andrewnorton.id.au/2026/07/14/the-fall-and-rise-of-postgraduate-commonwealth-supported-places/))

## Regulator
- **Clare signals legislation to give TEQSA direct fining power over universities without requiring court action; Royal Commission expected to surface "horrific evidence" of antisemitism on campus (12 July).** In a Sky News Sunday Agenda interview, Minister Clare announced plans to "introduce legislation to give the regulator more powers over the coming months" — enabling TEQSA to issue direct financial penalties to universities for antisemitism-related failures rather than pursuing court proceedings. Clare said he expects the Royal Commission on antisemitism at universities to surface incidents where Jewish students were called "baby killers" and subjected to systematic "abuse, intimidation, and harassment." The announcement extends the 13 July governance and anti-racism standard package, which mandated annual TEQSA reporting and formal complaint processes but stopped short of direct penalty powers — the gap Clare's foreshadowed legislation would close. ([Clare, 12 July](https://jasonclare.com.au/media/television-interview-with-andrew-clennell-sky-news-sunday-agenda-sunday-12-july-2026/))
